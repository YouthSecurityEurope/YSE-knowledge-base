    Getting Started with Microsoft Teams

    Audience: YSE staff and volunteers with Microsoft Teams access
    Last updated: June 2026

    What Is Microsoft Teams?

    Microsoft Teams is a collaboration platform for chat, video calls, and file sharing. Within YSE it is used for internal instant messaging and online meetings. Think of it as the Microsoft equivalent of Google Meet + a persistent group chat, all in one place.

    New to Teams? Start here.

    This guide covers the basics you need to communicate with colleagues. For step-by-step login instructions, see Logging In to Microsoft Teams.

    The Teams Interface at a Glance

    When you open Teams you will see a sidebar on the left with the main navigation:

    Icon What it does
    Chat One-on-one and group direct messages
    Teams Shared workspaces with channels (topic-based threads)
    Calendar Your schedule and meeting links
    Calls Voice and video calls
    Files Files shared with you across Teams and SharePoint

    The search bar at the top is the fastest way to find a person, a message, or a file.

    Messaging a Colleague

    Start a new chat

    1. Click Chat in the left sidebar.
    2. Click the pencil/compose icon (New Chat) near the top.
    3. In the To: field, type the name or email of the person you want to reach. YSE members will appear in the suggestions as you type.
    4. Type your message in the text box at the bottom and press Enter (or click the send button) to send.

    Search by name

    You do not need to remember a colleague's email address. Type their first name and Teams will find them within the YSE directory.

    Reply in an existing chat

    Click on any existing conversation in the Chat list. Your new message goes in the text box at the bottom.

    Group Chats

    To message more than one person at once:

    1. Start a new chat as above.
    2. In the To: field, add multiple names; Teams will turn the conversation into a group chat automatically.
    3. You can optionally give the group chat a name by clicking the pencil icon next to the participants at the top.

    Making and Joining Calls

    Start a call from a chat

    Inside any chat (one-on-one or group), use the icons in the top-right corner:

    • Camera icon → start a video call
    • Phone icon → start an audio-only call

    The other participants will receive a pop-up notification to join.

    Join a scheduled meeting

    Scheduled meetings appear in your Calendar tab. Click the meeting and then Join to enter the call. See the Google Calendar section below for meetings scheduled via Google.

    Teams and Channels

    A Team is a shared workspace for a group of people (e.g. a project team or a committee). Each Team contains one or more channels: topic-based threads where messages and files are organized.

    To browse Teams you have been added to, click Teams in the left sidebar. To post a message in a channel, open the channel and use the text box at the bottom.

    Note

    Access to specific Teams and channels is managed by administrators. If you need to be added to a Team, contact support@youthsecurity.org.

    Recording and Transcribing Meetings

    Teams can record meetings and generate an automatic transcript. Both features are available during any call or scheduled meeting.

    Start a recording

    1. Join a Teams meeting.
    2. In the meeting controls bar at the top (or bottom, depending on your layout), click the More menu (...).
    3. Select Start recording.

    All participants are notified that the meeting is being recorded. The recording begins immediately.

    Inform participants before recording

    Recording a meeting without the knowledge of participants may have legal implications depending on the jurisdiction. Always inform everyone at the start of the call that you intend to record.

    Start a transcription

    Transcription runs independently of the recording; you can have one without the other.

    1. In the meeting controls, click the More menu (...).
    2. Select Start transcription.

    A live transcript panel opens on the right side of the screen, showing what each participant says attributed by name.

    Stop recording or transcription

    Open the More menu (...) again and select Stop recording or Stop transcription.

    Where to find the recording and transcript after the meeting

    When the meeting ends, Teams processes the recording and transcript automatically. They are saved to Microsoft Stream and are accessible from the meeting chat:

    1. Open Chat in the left sidebar and find the meeting conversation.
    2. The recording appears as a playable video card in the chat, and the transcript is available as a downloadable .vtt file or viewable inline.

    For scheduled meetings, you can also find them in the Calendar tab: click the past meeting entry and check the Recordings tab.

    Processing time

    Recordings and transcripts may take a few minutes to appear after the meeting ends, depending on the length of the call.

    Scheduling Teams Meetings from Google Calendar

    YSE Google Calendar includes a Microsoft Teams add-on that lets you create Teams meeting links directly from a Calendar event, without ever opening Teams.

    Create a Teams meeting in Google Calendar

    1. Open Google Calendar.
    2. Click on a time slot or click + Create to open a new event.
    3. In the event editor, look for the "Add conferencing" dropdown (or a dedicated "Microsoft Teams meeting" button in the sidebar on the right).
    4. Select Microsoft Teams meeting from the options.
    5. A Teams meeting link is automatically generated and inserted into the event description.
    6. Add your guests, set the time, and click Save. Guests will see the Teams link in their calendar invite.

    The Teams link works for everyone

    Guests do not need a YSE Microsoft account to join a Teams meeting via a link; they can join as external guests directly from their browser.

    Join a Teams meeting from Google Calendar

    1. Open the event in Google Calendar.
    2. Click the Microsoft Teams meeting link in the event description.
    3. Your browser will open the Teams web app (or the desktop app if installed) and place you in the meeting lobby.
    4. Click Join now once the host admits you.

    Don't see the Teams option in Calendar?

    The Teams add-on must be enabled for your Google Workspace account. If you do not see the option, contact support@youthsecurity.org so the admin can enable it.

    Useful Keyboard Shortcuts

    Shortcut Action
    Ctrl + E / Cmd + E Focus the search bar
    Ctrl + N / Cmd + N New chat
    Ctrl + Shift + M / Cmd + Shift + M Mute/unmute in a call
    Ctrl + Shift + O / Cmd + Shift + O Turn camera on/off in a call
    Ctrl + / View all keyboard shortcuts

    Need Help?

    For login issues or account provisioning, contact support@youthsecurity.org.
    For general Teams questions, Microsoft's own documentation is available at support.microsoft.com.
